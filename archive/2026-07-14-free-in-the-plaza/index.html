Free in the Plaza

Free music in the plaza, a classic in the cemetery, and a slow day west of Lincoln.

Hi, this is one of those rare weeks where the best things to do are also the free ones. Grand Performances has three nights in a row at California Plaza that cost nothing, starting with Bastille Day tonight, and the water court downtown is about as pretty as summer gets after dark.

We are also cooking up the next Haps Club dinner, so if you want first word on it, or just a plus one for any of the picks below, come hang in our WhatsApp group. Now, this week.

The lead pick · Tonight

Bastille Day, free at California Plaza

French pop, a night market, and the plaza at its best.

Tue Jul 14 · 5–10 PM · California Plaza, 350 S Grand Ave, DTLA · free with RSVP

For the second year, the Consulate General of France teams up with Grand Performances for an open-air Bastille Day, a French market and a free concert in the heart of downtown. The bill is real: Sexion d'Assaut's Black M, Eurovision favorite Barbara Pravi, and French Touch pioneer Joachim Garraud closing on the decks. RSVP free.

The free week · Friday

Todos Somos Sagrados, an evening of flor y canto

Poetry from Boyle Heights, under the downtown sky.

Fri Jul 17 · 6–9 PM · California Plaza, 350 S Grand Ave, DTLA · free with RSVP

Poet and attorney Rey M. Rodriguez marks his debut collection with a night of readings honoring the women and organizers of Boyle Heights, joined by Lorna Dee Cervantes, Aideed Medina and more, with music from the all-female ensemble Las Chorizeras. It is a quieter, warmer side of the plaza. RSVP free.

The free week · Saturday

WONDER-FULL, a Stevie Wonder dance party

DJ Spinna, an outdoor plaza, and Songs in the Key of Life at 50.

Sat Jul 18 · 6–10 PM · California Plaza, 350 S Grand Ave, DTLA · free with RSVP

This is the one I would clear the calendar for. Brooklyn's DJ Spinna brings his long-running WONDER-FULL tribute, an inter-generational dance party built entirely around Stevie's catalogue, remixes and productions, the year his masterpiece Songs in the Key of Life turns 50. DJ Monalisa opens at 6, Spinna takes it to 10. Grab your spot.

The weekend · Saturday

Rosemary's Baby at Cinespia

A creeping classic on a lawn full of blankets and strangers.

Sat Jul 18 · gates 7:15 PM, film 9 PM · Hollywood Forever Cemetery · ticketed

If you would rather lie back than dance, Cinespia projects Polanski's slow-burn horror onto the mausoleum wall while the crowd spreads out across the Fairbanks Lawn. Come early with a picnic and low chairs, stay for the DJ sets and photo booth that bracket the film. It tends to sell out, so grab seats before the weekend. Tickets and the full how-to.

AWOL · Always West of Lincoln · Santa Monica

Wednesday market on Arizona Ave

Sixty-plus farms, the chefs' market, a Santa Monica institution.

Wed Jul 15 · 8 AM–1 PM · 155–199 Arizona Ave, Santa Monica · free entry

The downtown Wednesday market, running since 1981, is where a lot of LA's best kitchens actually shop, over sixty California farms deep. Go early with a tote and a coffee, taste your way down the rows, and build the week's cooking around whatever is ripe. Hours and directions.

AWOL · Santa Monica

The Santa Monica stairs, before it gets hot

A free, brutal, oddly social workout above the canyon.

Any morning · Adelaide Dr & 4th St, Santa Monica · free, street parking

Two public staircases drop into Santa Monica Canyon off Adelaide, and on any given morning they are full of regulars grinding out sets with the ocean at their backs. Go early for shade and parking, bring water, and do as many rounds as your legs allow. Find the stairs.

AWOL · Santa Monica

Sunset picnic at the Annenberg Beach House

The old Marion Davies estate, now everyone's backyard on the sand.

Thu Jul 16 · 5–8 PM · 415 Pacific Coast Hwy, Santa Monica · free

The Annenberg Community Beach House is a rare thing, a grand old beachfront estate the public actually gets to use, with volleyball courts, a splash pad and lawns right on the sand. Thursday evening they host a free sunset picnic, so pack a blanket and dinner and watch the light go down over the water.

That's the week. Free where it counts, coastal where it matters. If you make it to one of these, tell me what you thought. — Michael
