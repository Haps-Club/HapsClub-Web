● Tuesday, July 7, 2026 · Deep summer

Downtown Nights, Westside Days

Free music in the plaza, a classic in the cemetery, and a slow day west of Lincoln.

The fireworks are packed away and the city gets to exhale. This is the good, unhurried stretch of summer, when the best nights are mostly free and the days drift toward the water. A free DJ night downtown, Pulp Fiction under the stars at the cemetery, and a slow coastal day that ends at the Getty Villa.

The lead pick · This Friday

The Art of the Crossfade, at Grand Performances

A turntable as a bridge, free in the plaza, as the sun goes down.

Fri Jul 10 · 6–9 PM · California Plaza, 350 S Grand Ave, DTLA · free with RSVP

USC professor and MacArthur fellow Josh Kun teams up with DJ and producer J.PERIOD, the mind behind The Live Mixtape, for a night about the humble crossfader, the slider that lets two songs blend without erasing either. Part live mix, part cultural conversation, it kicks off Grand Performances’ 40th season of free outdoor concerts in downtown’s prettiest water-court plaza. Bring a friend and a light jacket. RSVP free.

The weekend · Saturday

Pulp Fiction at Cinespia

A Royale with cheese, a lawn full of strangers, a perfect film.

Sat Jul 11 · gates 7:15 PM, film 9 PM · Hollywood Forever Cemetery · ticketed

Cinespia’s 25th season rolls on with Tarantino’s coolest, projected onto a mausoleum wall as the crowd spreads blankets across the Fairbanks Lawn. Come early, pack a picnic and low chairs, and stay for the DJ sets and photo booth that bookend the film. It sells out, so grab seats before Saturday. Get tickets and the full how-to.

The weekend · Sunday

Sunday at Smorgasburg LA

A hundred vendors, one beer garden, no plan required.

Sun Jul 12 · 10 AM–4 PM · ROW DTLA, 777 S Alameda St · free entry, family friendly

The largest open-air food market in the country sets up every Sunday at ROW DTLA, nearly a hundred small vendors deep. Go hungry and grazing: tacos from Tacos 1986, smoked everything, micheladas, and a shaded beer garden to regroup in. Leave the dog at home, bring an appetite. See this week’s vendor list.

AWOL · Always West of Lincoln · Venice

Breakfast at Gjusta

The bakery that turned a Venice warehouse into a temple.

Open daily · 320 Sunset Ave, Venice · bakery, deli, coffee

Start the day where the neighborhood does. Gjusta is a sprawling, ingredient-driven bakery and deli a few blocks off the boardwalk, all morning buns, smoked fish, and bread baked on site. Order at the counter, fight for a patio seat, and let it set the pace for a slow westside day. Hours and directions.

AWOL · Marina del Rey

Paddle out at Mother’s Beach

Flat, calm water, the gentlest way to get on the ocean.

Any morning · Marina “Mother’s” Beach, Marina del Rey · rentals on site

If the open ocean feels like a lot, the harbor’s protected lagoon is the move. Mother’s Beach is glassy and shallow, with kayak and paddleboard rentals right there, so a first-timer and a regular can both have a good morning. Go early for calm water and easy parking, then dry off over lunch on the marina.

AWOL · Pacific Palisades

An afternoon at the Getty Villa

A Roman country house on a bluff over the Pacific.

Wed–Mon, 10 AM–5 PM (closed Tue) · 17985 Pacific Coast Hwy · free, timed reservation

End the day in antiquity. The Villa’s Greek and Roman galleries, reflecting pools and herb gardens are free with a timed reservation, and on now is The Egyptian Book of the Dead, a rare gathering of ancient funerary manuscripts running through November. Reserve ahead, and note parking is paid. Book a free entry time.

That’s the week. Free where it counts, coastal where it matters. If you make it to one of these, tell me what you thought. — Michael & the Haps Club community
