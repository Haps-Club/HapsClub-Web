The Longest Day

Our open-air solstice party in West Adams lands Saturday — and the whole city is dressing up for the longest day of the year.

Saturday is the summer solstice — the longest evening of the year — and we are spending it in a West Adams garden. Here is the one we have been building toward, plus the rest of the city worth folding into the same Saturday.

Haps Club · Saturday, June 20

Summer Solstice in the Garden

Haps Club and Twentieth Street Garden are throwing an open-air solstice party in West Adams on the longest evening of the year. BBQ smoked and slow-roasted on site, batched cocktails, beer and wine, a complimentary drink when you arrive, and live music while golden hour stretches out as long as it gets all year. Early-bird tickets are $10, and they tend to go before the night does. If you came out to our Cinco de Mayo party, consider this your nudge to do it again in the garden.

📍 Twentieth Street Garden · West Adams · Saturday, June 20 · 5–9 PM · Early bird $10

Free · Saturday, June 20

Make a full day of it: LACMA opens the Geffen Galleries

LACMA opens its new David Geffen Galleries that same Saturday with a free block party on Wilshire — live music, food, and Jeffrey Deitch's Art Parade — an easy afternoon stop before you head west for the garden.

📍 LACMA · 5905 Wilshire Blvd, Mid-Wilshire · Saturday, June 20 · Free

Music · Thursday–Saturday, June 19–20

Two more for solstice weekend

If you want a night out before Saturday: Grammy-winning Motown legend Thelma Houston plays two nights at Catalina in Hollywood over Juneteenth weekend (doors 7, show 8:30), and vocalist Vivian Sessoms closes the Broad Stage season in Santa Monica on Saturday with a Black Box set built around the women of 1970s disco.

📍 Catalina Jazz Club, Hollywood · June 19–20  ·  The Broad Stage, Santa Monica · June 20
