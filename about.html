About Haps Club

A weekly guide to LA
that respects your time.

Haps Club is a short, curated email and homepage of what's actually worth doing in Los Angeles this week — written by someone who actually goes.

What you get

Every Friday morning, one email lands in your inbox. It tells you what's on tonight, what to plan for the weekend, and a few things worth blocking off your calendar further out. No top-ten lists, no SEO bait, no "37 things to do this weekend in LA." Just a handful of picks I've vetted, with the address, the time, and a direct link to the venue.

The website is the same content, kept up-to-date so you can land here midweek and still find your night. Cards link straight to the source — no aggregators, no review sites — so you're one tap away from booking, not three.

How I pick

I live in LA. I go to the things. The bar I'm sending you to is a bar I've sat at; the patio I'm telling you to book is one I've eaten on. Where a venue is too new for me to have visited, I'll say so plainly and tell you what's known.

I don't take payment for placement. The only sponsored content is clearly labeled "Sponsored," appears at the bottom of the email, and never crosses into the picks. If you're a venue or event hoping to be featured, the only way in is to be good — and the way to tell me is below.

What this isn't

This isn't a comprehensive listings site — Time Out exists, the LA Times exists, you have Google. This is the opposite: a small, opinionated edit. If a Friday email has six things in it, it's because six things are worth your Friday.

It's also not a social club, a paid membership, or anything that requires you to buy a ticket. It's a free email. The "Become a member" framing on the homepage is just our way of saying: sign up, you're in.
